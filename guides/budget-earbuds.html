How to choose good wireless earbuds on a budget

Cheap wireless earbuds have improved a lot over the years. Here's what I think is actually worth paying attention to before buying a pair.

Penny Picks · Updated July 2026 · 9 min read

A few years ago, buying cheap wireless earbuds usually meant poor sound quality and constant connection problems. Thankfully, that's no longer true. Even budget earbuds can sound good if you choose carefully. The difficult part is knowing which features are useful and which ones are mostly there to make the box look impressive.

Active noise cancellation: check if it's "hybrid," not just "ANC"

Active Noise Cancellation sounds great, but not every budget pair does it well. If you travel a lot or use public transport, decent ANC is definitely worth paying extra for. Otherwise, a comfortable fit can block plenty of outside noise on its own.

Battery life numbers are almost always "total with the case"

Battery life is another thing to check. Companies often mention the total battery including the charging case, but the number that matters is how long the earbuds themselves last on a single charge.

Codec support matters only if your source matters

Bluetooth stability is something people forget until it becomes a problem. A reliable connection makes a much bigger difference than having lots of extra features you'll never use.

Don't buy earbuds just because they have huge drivers or fancy marketing terms. The tuning of the sound is usually more important than the driver size itself.

If you're mainly using them for college, music, YouTube, and phone calls, focus on comfort, battery life, and a stable connection. Those are the things you'll notice every day.

What separates a good $50 pair from a disappointing $20 pair

  • An IP rating. IPX4 or better means sweat and light rain won't kill them — worth prioritizing if you plan to work out in them.
  • A companion app with real EQ control. A basic three-band or preset EQ can meaningfully fix a bass-heavy or thin default tuning.
  • Established review history. Extremely cheap listings with inflated battery claims and no meaningful review history are the most common source of disappointment in this category — a slightly higher price from a known brand is often the safer bet.
  • Wired charging at minimum; wireless charging is a bonus, not a requirement at this price point.

Frequently asked questions

Do budget earbuds work well for phone calls?

My advice is simple: compare a few options, read genuine user reviews, and don't assume the most expensive pair under your budget is automatically the best choice.

Is multipoint Bluetooth worth looking for?

If you regularly switch between a phone and a laptop, yes — multipoint lets the earbuds stay connected to both and switch automatically, which is genuinely convenient. If you only ever use them with one device, it's not worth prioritizing.

Why do some $25 earbuds sound better than $45 ones?

Tuning philosophy matters more than the price tag within this range. A well-tuned driver with a balanced frequency response often sounds better than a larger driver with an exaggerated, unbalanced bass boost — read for mentions of tuning quality, not just driver size, before deciding.
